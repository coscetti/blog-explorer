Project

Analysing 10+ Years of R Blogs

We like beautiful data products.

Our clients often struggle to extract meaningful information from free text documents. In machine learning and natural language processing, a topic model is a type of statistical model for discovering the abstract topics that occur in a collection of documents. Topic modeling is a frequently used text-mining tool for discovery of hidden semantic structures in a collection of documents.

Data

R-Bloggers is an aggregator for blogs about R and has been around for more than a decade. It is very interesting to see how the R scene has developed over the last 10 years. What topics were talked about in the past, what are people writing about today? Would we see the rise of Shiny, the tidyverse, Rmarkdown, CRAN releases, conference announcements?

We scraped the blog data from R-Bloggers from their public archives by.

  1. Obtain a list of links to articles published on R-Bloggers
  2. Download each article from the link collection
  3. Parse each document and combined them into a single dataframe

For each document we also collected meta information such as blog title, publication date, author and URLs to the original blog.
